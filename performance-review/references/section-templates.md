# Section Templates — Quarterly Conversation

Writing templates for each review section. Used during Phase 4 (Draft All Sections) to ensure consistent structure, tone, and length.

## General Principles

- **First person** throughout — "I led..." not "Jesse led..."
- **Impact-led** — lead with what changed, not what you did
- **Specific** — name projects, metrics, stakeholders where possible
- **Honest** — don't inflate; understatement reads as confidence
- **No corporate filler** — skip "synergy," "leverage," "align around"

---

## Keep / Continue

**Purpose**: Highlight what went well and should continue. This is your strongest section — lead with your best work.

**Length**: 3-5 bullet points

**Tone**: Confident but not boastful. Let the evidence speak.

**Pattern**: `[Action/behavior] → [impact/outcome]`

**Example bullets**:
- Drove the [Feature X] initiative from spec through delivery, resulting in [outcome]. Tight coordination with [team] kept the timeline on track.
- Maintained backlog health across [N] active epics — sprint commitments hit [X]% completion rate this quarter.
- Proactively identified [issue] before it reached production. Escalated early, coordinated the fix with [team], resolved in [timeframe].

**Evidence sources**: Resolved Jira tickets, epic completions, Slack wins/announcements, positive stakeholder threads

---

## Stop

**Purpose**: Self-aware reflection on what didn't work. Shows growth mindset. Frame constructively — not self-flagellation, but honest assessment.

**Length**: 2-3 items

**Tone**: Candid, constructive. Acknowledge the cost without dwelling.

**Pattern**: `[What happened] → [cost/impact] → [what I'd do differently]`

**Example bullets**:
- Spent too long on [task/initiative] before recognizing it wasn't the right priority. Cost was [X weeks / delayed Y]. Going forward, I'll timebox exploratory work and checkpoint sooner.
- Didn't push back early enough on [scope creep / unclear requirement]. Led to [rework / missed deadline]. Need to be more direct about feasibility during planning.

**Evidence sources**: Stale tickets, rework patterns, long cycle times, Slack frustration threads, blockers that lasted too long

---

## Start

**Purpose**: Forward-looking commitments. Shows strategic thinking and self-direction.

**Length**: 2-3 items

**Tone**: Actionable, specific. Each item should be something you can actually do next quarter.

**Pattern**: `[Do X] → [to achieve Y]`

**Example bullets**:
- Start running structured sprint retros with the team to surface process friction earlier.
- Begin tracking [metric] as a leading indicator for [outcome] — gives us earlier signal on [risk area].
- Invest time in [skill/area] to close a gap that showed up this quarter (specifically around [context]).

**Evidence sources**: Gaps identified in Stop section, team feedback, recurring problems, areas where you felt underprepared

---

## Employee Remarks

**Purpose**: Overall self-assessment with a rating. Synthesizes the quarter into a coherent narrative.

**Length**: 2-4 sentences + rating selection

**Tone**: Balanced, reflective. Neither falsely modest nor inflated.

**Pattern**: `[Overall assessment] + [strongest contribution] + [area for growth] + [rating]`

**Example**:
> This was a productive quarter focused on [theme]. The biggest win was [X], which [impact]. I underperformed on [Y] and plan to address it by [action]. Rating: ME — Meets Expectation.

**Rating selection guidance**:
- **EE (Exceeds Expectation)**: Delivered beyond scope, led initiatives outside your role, measurable outsized impact
- **ME (Meets Expectation)**: Delivered on commitments, handled responsibilities well, some areas for growth
- Use EE sparingly and only with strong evidence. ME is not a bad rating.

---

## Development Plans

**Purpose**: Concrete growth goals for the next quarter (or longer). Ties to career trajectory, not just current role.

**Length**: 1-3 items with timeframes

**Tone**: Specific, measurable where possible. Not aspirational fluff.

**Pattern**: `[Skill/area] — [specific action] — [timeframe] — [how I'll know it worked]`

**Example items**:
- **Technical depth**: Complete [course/certification/project] by end of Q2. Success = able to [specific capability].
- **Leadership**: Take ownership of [cross-team initiative] next quarter. Success = [deliverable] shipped with [team] aligned.
- **Domain expertise**: Deepen understanding of [area] by [action — shadowing, reading, attending]. Apply to [upcoming project].

**Evidence sources**: Gaps from Stop section, feedback from supervisor, career goals, upcoming challenges that require new skills
